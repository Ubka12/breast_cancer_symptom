Common breast changes

Not a complete list, these are examples people often mention. If something feels unusual for you, use the checker and consider medical advice.

  • New lump or thickening (breast or underarm)
  • Change in size, shape, or feel
  • Redness, rash, heat, or swelling
  • Dimpling or puckering of the skin
  • Nipple changes (inversion, new crusting)
  • Nipple discharge (especially clear or bloody)
  • Persistent pain or new tenderness
  • Skin that looks like “peau d’orange” (orange-peel texture)

Not every change is cancer

Many breast changes are normal or harmless. The checker helps you describe what’s going on so you can decide next steps with more confidence.

Know your normal

Breasts vary across the month and over time. What matters is noticing what’s new for you or doesn’t settle.

When to seek medical advice

If you notice a new lump, skin changes that don’t go away, nipple changes or discharge, or anything that feels unusual for you, please contact your GP or NHS 111. If you’re unsure, it’s okay to ask earlier advice is always better.
