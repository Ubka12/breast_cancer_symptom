Breast Self-Check Guide

Checking your breasts regularly can help you notice changes early. This simple routine takes just a few minutes each month. It’s not about diagnosing, it’s about understanding what’s normal for you.

Your monthly routine (5 steps)

  1. 1

    Look in the mirror

    Stand with shoulders straight and hands on hips. Notice shape, size, or skin changes dimpling, puckering, redness, or rashes.

  2. 2

    Raise your arms

    Lift both arms and look again for the same changes. Check that both sides move evenly.

  3. 3

    Check the nipples

    Look for new inversion, crusting, or any fluid especially clear or bloody discharge.

  4. 4

    Feel standing or sitting

    Using the pads of three fingers, move in small circles covering the whole breast and underarm. Use light, then medium, then firm pressure.

  5. 5

    Feel lying down

    Place a pillow under one shoulder and your hand behind your head. Repeat the circular motions across the entire breast and armpit area.

Pick a regular time

Once a month is enough. If you have periods, check a few days after they end when breasts are less tender.

Know your normal

Breasts naturally change over time. You’re looking for what’s new for you or doesn’t settle.

No perfect technique

Don’t stress about doing it “right”. Being consistent matters more than being precise.

If you notice something unusual

New lump or thickening, skin that dimples, nipple changes or discharge, redness or swelling that doesn’t go away — please contact your GP or NHS 111. Most changes are not cancer, but it’s always okay to ask.
